Mobile Youth Work &
Child Advocacy

Our flagship outreach project — reaching the most vulnerable children and youth in Kisumu through integrated activities that address their most critical needs.

Project Overview

Reaching Children Where They Are

Mobile Youth Work and Child Advocacy is UCDP's flagship project — a wide-reaching, community-embedded programme designed to identify, support, and advocate for the most vulnerable children and young people in Kisumu County and beyond.

The project is "mobile" in the truest sense — our teams go into the community, to the streets, to schools, and to homes. We do not wait for children to come to us. We go to them, build relationships with them, and create pathways that connect them to safety, education, healthcare, and opportunity.

Under this project, UCDP runs integrated activities, each addressing a specific dimension of vulnerability — from basic needs like food and sanitary products, to long-term interventions like school sponsorship, reintegration, and policy advocacy.

What We Do Under This Project

Integrated Activities, One Mission

Each activity targets a specific need — together they form a comprehensive safety net for the most vulnerable children in our community.

🩸

Sanitary Pads Distribution

Distributing sanitary pads to girls under 18 across all sub-counties in Kisumu County — so no girl ever misses school because of her period.

Learn More →
🍚

Relief Food Distribution

Providing nutritional support to malnourished children aged 5 and below, identified through clinical assessment at our Community Clinic.

Learn More →
🎓

Senior School Sponsorship

Sponsoring school fees for needy students across Kisumu County throughout their full 4 years of senior school education.

Learn More →
🤲

Street Work & Reintegration

Trained social workers and volunteers reach out to street children — building trust and guiding them towards safe, stable lives.

Safe House for Street Girls

A secure, nurturing refuge for girls from the streets — including mothers with their children, some of them second-generation street children.
